In today's world, travellers don't call hotels directly. They open Booking.com, Agoda or MakeMyTrip. If your hotel isn't there — you simply don't exist for them. OTA listing is not optional. It is the foundation of every rupee your hotel earns from online bookings.

What is an OTA and why does it matter?

An Online Travel Agency (OTA) is a website or app where travellers search, compare and book hotels. Think of it as a giant digital marketplace — just like Daraz for products, but for hotel rooms. When a tourist plans a trip to Pokhara or Kathmandu, the first thing they do is open Booking.com and type in their destination. Your hotel needs to appear in those search results.

Nepal welcomed over 1 million international tourists in 2023. More than 72% of all hotel bookings in South Asia now happen through OTAs. Hotels not listed on major OTAs miss most of this demand entirely.

7 reasons why OTA listing directly increases your revenue

🌍

Global Reach Instantly

A traveller in Germany planning a Nepal trip finds your hotel on Booking.com. Without OTAs, they would never discover you — no matter how beautiful your property is.

📊

Higher Occupancy

Hotels listed on 5+ OTAs see an average 28% higher occupancy compared to those listed on 1–2 platforms. More channels = more eyes = more bookings.

⭐

Reviews Build Trust

OTA guest reviews are your most powerful marketing tool. A hotel with 200+ reviews and a 8.5+ score consistently outranks competitors and commands higher prices.

💳

Zero Upfront Cost

You only pay a commission when a booking is made. No listing fees, no monthly charges. The OTA takes a percentage (typically 15–18%) only after you earn.

📱

Mobile Travellers

67% of travellers now book via mobile. OTA apps handle the entire booking flow, payment processing and customer support — so you don't have to.

🔄

Last-Minute Fill

OTAs excel at selling last-minute inventory. Instead of having empty rooms tonight, smart OTA pricing can fill them at a profitable rate up to the last hour.
